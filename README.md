This thesis investigates the fusion of multiple semantically related textual prompts as independent evidence sources to improve robust detection of rare and unseen object categories in open-vocabulary object detection (OVD). Current Vision Language Models and OVD approaches rely on single textual descriptions or simple ensemble averaging, which often fail to capture the semantic richness and complementarity of synonymous descriptions. We propose a systematic framework grounded in Information Fusion principles that treats multiple prompts as independent evidence sources, employing advanced fusion strategies to aggregate conflicting and complementary semantic information while managing uncertainty.  
  
This work addresses six interconnected research questions spanning semantic evidence fusion, fusion strategy optimization, uncertainty and conflict modelling, redundancy-complementarity analysis, prior aware fusion, and robustness to semantic perturbations. The proposed approach is validated on LVIS v1.0 and COCO-ZSD datasets using WordNet-derived synonyms. We expect significant improvements in detection of rare objects and unseen categories while maintaining strong performance on base classes. The research contributes to both the Information Fusion and Computer Vision communities by formalizing multi-source semantic fusion for object detection and providing a methodologically rigorous framework for evidence aggregation.
